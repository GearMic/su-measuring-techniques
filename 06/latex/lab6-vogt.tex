\section{Theory}
A transmission line can be viewed as a series of many infinitesimal LRC circuits.
These combine to a total resistance $R$, inductance $L$, capacitance $C$ and transconductance $G$.
The transmission line's impedance, i.\,e. the frequency-dependent ratio of voltage to current,
is then
\begin{equation*}
  Z(\omega) = \sqrt\frac{j\omega L+R}{j\omega C+G}
\end{equation*}
